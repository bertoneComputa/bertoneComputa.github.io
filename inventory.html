U.S. Air Force Aircraft

Fighter Aircraft

F-15 Eagle

The F-15 Eagle is an all-weather, extremely maneuverable, tactical fighter designed to permit the Air Force to gain and maintain air supremacy over the battlefield.

F-16 Fighting Falcon

The F-16 Fighting Falcon is a compact, multi-role fighter aircraft, proven in air-to-air combat and air-to-surface attack, providing a high-performance weapon system for the U.S. and allies.

F-22 Raptor

The F-22 Raptor combines stealth, supercruise, maneuverability, and integrated avionics for unmatched warfighting capabilities in both air-to-air and air-to-ground missions.

F-35A Lightning II

The F-35A is the U.S. Air Force’s latest fifth-generation fighter, providing advanced stealth and situational awareness while delivering next-generation performance.

Helicopter Aircraft

CV-22 Osprey

The CV-22 Osprey is a tiltrotor aircraft that merges vertical takeoff, hover, and landing with the range and speed of a turboprop, enabling long-range operations.

HH-60G Pave Hawk

The HH-60G Pave Hawk specializes in day/night personnel recovery operations, often behind hostile lines, to rescue isolated individuals during war or crisis.

UH-1N Huey

The UH-1N is a light-lift utility helicopter supporting missions like emergency security force transport, nuclear weapons convoy security, and distinguished visitor airlift.

Attack Aircraft

A-10C Thunderbolt II

The A-10C is the first U.S. Air Force aircraft dedicated to close air support of ground forces. It’s effective against all ground targets, including tanks and other armor.

AC-130J Ghostrider

The AC-130J is a modified C-130J featuring advanced avionics. It excels at close air support, air interdiction, and armed reconnaissance in hostile environments.

Bomber Aircraft

B-1B Lancer

The multi-mission B-1B carries the largest conventional payload of guided and unguided weapons in the Air Force, providing massive firepower anywhere in the world.

B-2 Spirit

The B-2 Spirit is a multi-role stealth bomber capable of delivering both conventional and nuclear weapons, penetrating sophisticated defenses to strike globally.

B-21 Raider

The B-21 Raider is the Air Force’s next-generation stealth bomber, designed to deliver both conventional and nuclear munitions with enhanced survivability.

B-52H Stratofortress

The long-range B-52H can deliver a variety of weapons, including nuclear or precision-guided conventional munitions, and has a 70-year track record of reliability.

AWACS Aircraft

E-3 Sentry (AWACS)

The E-3 Sentry is an airborne warning and control system with an integrated C2 battle management, surveillance, target detection, and tracking platform.

E-4B

The E-4B serves as the National Airborne Operations Center, ensuring survivable command and control for national leadership in emergencies.

Tanker Aircraft

KC-10 Extender

The KC-10 Extender is an advanced aerial refueling and cargo aircraft, providing increased global mobility for U.S. armed forces.

KC-46A Pegasus

The KC-46A is the newest aerial refueling tanker, offering improved refueling, cargo, and aeromedical evacuation capabilities to support joint and allied forces.

KC-135 Stratotanker

The KC-135 Stratotanker provides the core aerial refueling capability for the U.S. Air Force and has done so for more than 60 years, extending the global reach of U.S. forces.

Transport Aircraft

C-5 Galaxy

The C-5M Super Galaxy is the largest Air Force transport aircraft, designed to carry oversized cargo and personnel globally, supporting military operations.

C-17 Globemaster III

The C-17 is a versatile cargo aircraft capable of rapid strategic delivery of troops and equipment directly to forward operating bases around the globe.

C-20

The C-20 is a twin-engine turbofan aircraft used to transport high-ranking government and DoD officials worldwide for special air missions.

C-21

The C-21 is a twin turbofan-engine aircraft used for passenger and cargo airlift. It can also be configured for aeromedical evacuation operations.

C-32

The C-32 provides safe, comfortable, and reliable transportation for U.S. leadership, including the Vice President, using call sign “Air Force Two.”

C-37A/B

The C-37A/B are twin-engine turbofan aircraft supporting worldwide special airlift missions for high-ranking government and DoD officials.

C-40 B/C

The C-40 B/C provides safe and reliable transportation for U.S. leaders. Its primary customers include combatant commanders, the Cabinet, and Congress.

C-130 Hercules

The C-130 performs tactical airlift, capable of operating from rough dirt strips, and is the prime transport for airdropping troops and equipment into hostile areas.

HC-130J Combat King II

The HC-130J is the only dedicated fixed-wing personnel recovery platform in the Air Force, providing combat search-and-rescue capabilities and helicopter refueling.

HC-130P/N King

The HC-130P/N “King” is also a dedicated personnel recovery platform, extending rescue operations with in-flight refueling of rescue helicopters.

MC-130H Combat Talon II

The MC-130H is used for infiltration, exfiltration, and resupply of special ops forces, along with helicopter refueling, typically in hostile or denied territory.

VC-25 (Air Force One)

The VC-25—commonly known as Air Force One—provides safe and reliable presidential airlift around the globe, equipped with specialized communication systems and security.

Reconnaissance Aircraft

MC-12W Liberty

The MC-12W is a medium- to low-altitude twin-engine turboprop, providing real-time intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance to ground forces.

RC-135S Cobra Ball

The RC-135S tracks ballistic missile flights by collecting measurement and signature intelligence, supporting national-level taskings on short notice.

E-8C Joint Stars

The E-8C is an airborne battle management, command and control, intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance platform, tracking ground movements to guide operations.

U-2S/TU-2S Dragon Lady

The U-2 provides high-altitude, all-weather surveillance and reconnaissance, day or night, in direct support of U.S. and allied forces.

Weather Reconnaissance

WC-135 Constant Phoenix

The WC-135 collects atmospheric samples to detect nuclear weapons tests, supporting the Limited Nuclear Test Ban Treaty of 1963.

WC-130 Hercules

The WC-130 is flown by the Air Force Reserve for weather reconnaissance, penetrating storms, hurricanes, and winter systems to gather vital data.

Trainer Aircraft

T-1A Jayhawk

The T-1A is a twin-engine jet trainer used in the advanced phase of specialized undergraduate pilot training, focusing on airlift and tanker tracks.

T-6A Texan II

The T-6A is a single-engine, two-seat primary trainer used to teach basic flight skills to Air Force and Navy student pilots.

T-38 Talon

The T-38 is a twin-engine, high-altitude, supersonic jet trainer used in a variety of roles, thanks to its high performance and proven safety record.

TG-16A Glider

The TG-16A is part of the U.S. Air Force Academy’s sailplane fleet, training cadets in basic soaring operations.

Electronic Warfare

EC-130H Compass Call

The EC-130H Compass Call disrupts enemy command and control communications, limiting adversary coordination essential for success in the battlefield.

Experimental Aircraft

X-37B Orbital Test Vehicle

The X-37B is an experimental test program to demonstrate technologies for a reliable, reusable, unmanned space test platform for the U.S. Air Force.

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV)

MQ-1B Predator

The MQ-1B Predator is an armed, multi-mission, medium-altitude, long-endurance remotely piloted aircraft used for intelligence collection and dynamic target engagement.

MQ-9 Reaper

The MQ-9 Reaper is a multi-mission, medium-altitude, long-endurance RPA primarily employed for dynamic execution of close air support and intelligence gathering.

RQ-4 Global Hawk

The RQ-4 Global Hawk is a high-altitude, long-endurance RPA with an integrated sensor suite, enabling global all-weather, day or night ISR.
