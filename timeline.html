1775

Birth of the Continental Army

The U.S. Army originated from the Continental Army, formed on June 14, 1775.

More Details

On June 14, 1775, the Continental Congress established the Army to fight Great Britain in the Revolutionary War. George Washington took command in July 1775, marking the formal birth of the U.S. Army.

1783

End of the Revolutionary War

After winning independence, the U.S. Army briefly demobilized but proved crucial on the frontier.

More Details

The signing of the Treaty of Paris (1783) ended the Revolutionary War. Although the Army’s size shrank significantly post-war, it remained the backbone of national defense.

1861–1865

The Civil War

A transformative conflict that revolutionized warfare and Army operations.

More Details

The Civil War accelerated innovations like rail logistics, telegraphs, and advanced weaponry. More than 2 million soldiers served in the Union Army, and the war solidified the federal government’s authority.

1917–1918

World War I

The U.S. quickly expanded its Army to join the global conflict.

More Details

The U.S. declared war on Germany in 1917. By 1918, over 4 million Americans had served, adopting modern artillery, trench warfare tactics, and combined arms strategies.

1941–1945

World War II

Massive mobilization cemented the Army's global leadership role.

More Details

Sparked by the attack on Pearl Harbor (1941), the Army led the fight across Europe and the Pacific, involving millions of personnel and pioneering amphibious assaults and airborne operations.

1950–1953

Korean War

A Cold War-era conflict testing U.S. readiness in Asia.

More Details

The Korean War underlined the Army’s need for rapid deployment and global presence. Although overshadowed by WWII and Vietnam, it heavily influenced future military doctrine.

1965–1973

Vietnam War

Helicopter warfare, counterinsurgency, and the shift to an All-Volunteer Force.

More Details

Significant controversies at home led to policy changes. With heavy involvement in Southeast Asia, the Army refined air mobility tactics and ended the draft in 1973, moving to a professional volunteer force.

1990–1991

Gulf War

Operation Desert Storm showcased advanced U.S. technology and coalition warfare.

More Details

In response to Iraq’s invasion of Kuwait, the U.S. Army joined a broad coalition. Precision-guided munitions, extensive aerial bombardment, and rapid armor thrusts were key to a swift victory.

2001–Present

Global War on Terror

Counterterrorism operations worldwide following 9/11.

More Details

Operations in Afghanistan and Iraq reshaped Army doctrine to emphasize counterinsurgency, special operations, and intelligence-driven missions.

Future

Modernization & Technology

Cyber warfare, AI, and robotics define the next frontier.

More Details

The Army invests in artificial intelligence, robotics, and next-gen weaponry to maintain a technological edge in increasingly complex security environments.
